# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers create intuitive, accessible, and visually compelling user experiences. They conduct user research, design interfaces, and validate designs through usability testing to ensure the product meets user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize insights into design requirements
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define and maintain design systems and style guides
- Collaborate with developers to ensure design implementation fidelity
- Validate designs through usability testing and user feedback
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Create user-centered designs that improve satisfaction and engagement
- Maintain design consistency across all product touchpoints
- Reduce usability issues and friction in user flows
- Balance user needs with business goals and technical constraints

### Typical Communication
- Weekly design reviews with product and engineering teams
- User research findings and design presentations
- Design specs and handoff documentation
- Feedback sessions during sprint demos

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze processes, document workflows, and ensure that technical implementations align with business objectives and deliver measurable value.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create process flows, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder sessions
- Validate that solutions meet business needs and acceptance criteria
- Support data analysis and business reporting needs

### Goals
- Ensure clear alignment between business needs and technical solutions
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements and specifications
- Improve process efficiency and business outcomes
- Enable data-driven decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user stories
- Process diagrams and workflow documentation
- Stakeholder meetings and requirement reviews
- Analysis reports and insights presentations

---

## Technical Lead

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction and architectural guidance for the team. They make key technical decisions, mentor developers, establish coding standards, and ensure the technical quality and scalability of solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Review and approve technical design decisions
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews
- Establish and enforce coding standards and best practices
- Identify and mitigate technical risks and debt
- Coordinate with other technical leads on cross-team dependencies
- Evaluate and recommend tools, frameworks, and technologies

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions are scalable, maintainable, and secure
- Build technical capabilities within the team
- Reduce technical debt and improve code quality
- Enable faster, safer delivery through good technical practices

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture diagrams
- Code review feedback and technical guidance
- Technical spike findings and recommendations
- Architecture review board presentations

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the quality strategy and testing approach for the project. They coordinate testing activities, establish quality standards, and ensure comprehensive test coverage to deliver reliable, high-quality software.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and testing strategies
- Plan and coordinate test activities across the team
- Create and maintain test plans and test cases
- Lead exploratory testing and identify edge cases
- Manage defect triage and prioritization
- Establish and track quality metrics
- Mentor team members on testing best practices
- Coordinate with automation engineers on test automation

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality software with minimal production defects
- Establish comprehensive test coverage (unit, integration, E2E)
- Reduce time to detect and resolve issues
- Build a culture of quality across the team

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and testing status reports
- Defect reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Quality reviews during sprint planning and retrospectives
- Testing guidance and best practices documentation

---

## Support/Operations Liaison

### Role Summary
Support/Operations Liaisons represent the operational perspective in the project. They ensure that solutions are operable, supportable, and observable in production, and serve as the bridge between engineering teams and support/operations teams.

### Responsibilities
- Represent operational requirements during planning and design
- Ensure solutions include proper monitoring, logging, and alerting
- Document operational procedures and runbooks
- Coordinate deployment and rollout strategies
- Facilitate incident response and post-incident reviews
- Gather feedback from support teams to inform product improvements
- Ensure knowledge transfer to support and operations teams

### Goals
- Minimize production incidents and operational toil
- Ensure rapid detection and resolution of issues
- Maintain clear operational documentation and runbooks
- Enable smooth handoffs between engineering and operations

### Typical Communication
- Operational readiness reviews and deployment checklists
- Runbooks and troubleshooting guides
- Incident reports and post-mortems
- On-call handoff documentation and escalation procedures

## Cross-Role Responsibilities and Onboarding Checklist

This section provides a template for clarifying interactions and responsibilities across roles during project onboarding.

### Project Onboarding Checklist Template

**For All Team Members:**
- [ ] Review project charter and understand project goals
- [ ] Understand your role and responsibilities in the project
- [ ] Identify your primary points of contact for each role
- [ ] Review the communication cadence and required meetings
- [ ] Access project documentation and tools (repos, boards, wikis)
- [ ] Understand the definition of done and quality standards

**Role-Specific Interactions:**

**Developers should:**
- [ ] Connect with Technical Lead for technical guidance and architecture questions
- [ ] Coordinate with UX/UI Designer for design implementation details
- [ ] Work with Business Analyst to clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
- [ ] Engage QA Lead for testing strategy and quality expectations
- [ ] Consult Support/Operations Liaison for operational requirements

**Product Managers should:**
- [ ] Align with Business Analyst on requirements prioritization
- [ ] Collaborate with UX/UI Designer on user experience strategy
- [ ] Partner with Technical Lead on technical feasibility and trade-offs
- [ ] Engage QA Lead on quality goals and acceptance criteria
- [ ] Coordinate with Support/Operations Liaison on operational impact

**Project Managers should:**
- [ ] Coordinate with all roles to establish timeline and dependencies
- [ ] Work with Technical Lead to identify technical risks
- [ ] Partner with QA Lead on testing timelines and release readiness
- [ ] Engage Support/Operations Liaison for deployment planning
- [ ] Facilitate cross-role alignment meetings

**UX/UI Designers should:**
- [ ] Collaborate with Product Managers on user needs and priorities
- [ ] Work with Business Analyst to understand business workflows
- [ ] Partner with Developers on design feasibility and implementation
- [ ] Engage QA Lead on usability testing approach

**Business Analysts should:**
- [ ] Partner with Product Managers on requirements definition
- [ ] Collaborate with UX/UI Designer on user experience requirements
- [ ] Work closely with Developers on technical implementation details
- [ ] Engage stakeholders for requirements validation

**Technical Leads should:**
- [ ] Mentor and guide Developers on technical decisions
- [ ] Collaborate with Product Managers on technical feasibility
- [ ] Partner with QA Lead on quality engineering practices
- [ ] Engage Support/Operations Liaison on operational architecture

**QA Leads should:**
- [ ] Coordinate with Developers on testability and test automation
- [ ] Partner with Technical Lead on quality standards and practices
- [ ] Work with Product Managers on acceptance criteria
- [ ] Engage Support/Operations Liaison on production validation

**Support/Operations Liaisons should:**
- [ ] Coordinate with Developers on operational requirements
- [ ] Partner with Technical Lead on system architecture and observability
- [ ] Work with QA Lead on production readiness testing
- [ ] Provide feedback to Product Managers on operational challenges

### Key Collaboration Patterns

**Requirements → Design → Implementation**
Business Analyst → UX/UI Designer → Developer → QA Lead

**Technical Decisions**
Technical Lead ↔ Developers ↔ QA Lead

**Release & Deployment**
Project Manager → QA Lead → Support/Operations Liaison → Developers

**Continuous Improvement**
Support/Operations Liaison → Product Manager → Business Analyst → Technical Lead
